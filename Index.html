Land

is the solid terrestrial surface of Earth not submerged by the ocean or another body of water. It makes up 29.2% of Earth's surface and includes all continents and islands. Earth's land surface is almost entirely covered by regolith, a layer of rock, soil, and minerals that forms the outer part of the crust. To read more click this.

Soil

is one of the essential components of land. It consists of particles of rock, sand and clay as well as organic material such as plant residues, soil-dwelling animals and organisms such as bacteria and fungi, along with the air and water in soil pores. To read more click this.

Degradation

is the process by which the quality and productivity of the land decline over time due to various factors, including human activities and natural processes. It can have a significant impact on ecosystems, agriculture, and the environment. To read more click this.

Desertification

is the process by which vegetation in drylands i.e. arid and semi-arid lands, such as grasslands or shrublands, decreases and eventually disappears.The concept does not refer to the physical expansion of existing deserts, but to the various processes that threaten to turn currently non-desert ecosystems into deserts.
